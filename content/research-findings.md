## Platform Intelligence

**TikTok is now a primary discovery channel — not optional.**
- TikTok as a wedding planning resource jumped from 15% → 25% of ALL couples year-over-year
- 30% of Gen Z couples (now the majority of the wedding market) use TikTok specifically to find vendors
- 40% of couples now ask photographers to capture "social-first" content
- Source: Zola 2026 First Look Report, The Knot 2026 Study

**Instagram algorithm hierarchy (confirmed 2026):**
Watch time / completion rate → DM shares ("sends per reach") → Saves → Likes
- Reels get ~2.25x the reach of single image posts
- Reels with >60% completion rate outperform weak-hook Reels by 5–10x
- Reels = reach engine. Carousels = save/authority engine. Use both intentionally.
- Source: Instagram (Mosseri, Jan 2025), Later, Sprout Social, DataSlayer

## Top-Performing Content Formats (Priority Order)

### 1. Venue Transformation Reels — HIGHEST REACH FORMAT
Before/after reveals of venue transformations consistently outperform all other formats for wedding planners on both TikTok and Reels. The built-in reveal structure holds watch time better than anything else.
- **Audience:** Target luxury client (desire-driven)
- **Metrics goal:** Watch time + Shares
- **Gap note:** Limited transformation footage in current portfolio — strategic priority to capture going forward
- Source: socialmon.ai, fullybookedvenue.com, later.com

### 2. Design "Ick" / Mistake Callout Content — HIGHEST SHARE FORMAT
Melissa Andre's most-engaged content explicitly uses this format. "Here are my wedding design icks" — calling out specific choices that date, cheapen, or genericify events. Fear of a generic wedding is a stronger short-term motivator than desire for a great one.
- **Audience:** Target luxury client (fear-driven)
- **Metrics goal:** Shares + Comments
- **Native fit:** This IS the "designed not decorated" philosophy in hook form
- Source: instagram.com/melissaandre/reel/C8DTwE3yZrI, theleap.co

### 3. Mood Board → Reality Carousels — HIGHEST SAVE FORMAT
Showing the concept-to-execution journey: mood board or design brief → how it translated into the actual event. This works even when the final event's budget was mid-range because the *thinking process* signals luxury taste.
- **Audience:** Target luxury client (desire-driven)
- **Metrics goal:** Saves (algorithm authority signal)
- **Portfolio gap solution:** This format elevates any past event through the design narrative
- Source: socialmon.ai, sourced Co, theplannersvault.com

### 4. Day-in-the-Life / POV Luxury Planner
"POV: Welcome to the world of luxury weddings" — showing the thinking, the site visits, the sourcing calls, the moment a design concept clicks. LLG Events (@llgevents) uses this explicitly and it drives follows + watch time.
- **Audience:** Both (desire-driven — couples want a planner whose taste matches theirs)
- **Metrics goal:** Watch time + Follows
- Source: tiktok.com/@llgevents, theleap.co

### 5. Specific Design Education — HIGHEST AUTHORITY FORMAT
Not vague tips but opinionated specifics: chair selection, linen logic, candle height relative to table width, the exact lighting setup that makes a room feel expensive. Vague tips don't get saved. Specific, counterintuitive design insights do.
- **High-performing topic categories:**
  - Chair selection (which chairs photograph expensively vs. cheaply)
  - Linen texture/color (what instantly elevates or dates a table)
  - Candle logic (height, density, flame count)
  - Tablescape layering (designed vs. arranged)
  - Florals (wild/organic vs. structured — which reads luxury in 2026)
  - Draping (when it elevates vs. when it reads banquet hall)
- **Audience:** Both (fear of wrong choices + desire for insider logic)
- **Metrics goal:** Saves
- Source: theleap.co (Melissa Andre interview), socialmon.ai

### 6. Color Story / Palette Naming Content
Creating and naming evocative palettes ("Venetian Dusk," "Moody Venetian Autumn") in carousels where 5–8 images share a color/texture/mood logic. Couples save these and return to them during planning. Positions the planner as a taste-maker.
- **Audience:** Target luxury client (desire-driven)
- **Metrics goal:** Saves + Shares
- Source: candicecoppola.com, socialmon.ai, theknot.com

### 7. Aspirational Zero-Text Reels (beauty-forward, music-driven)
Pure visual footage — no text overlays, just event footage set to music. Candice Coppola identifies this as a rising 2025 format as how-to content declines. Signals: "this is the level we work at."
- **Audience:** Target luxury client (desire-driven)
- **Metrics goal:** Watch time + Profile visits
- Source: candicecoppola.com

### 8. Trend Commentary ("What's over, what's next")
Reacting to wedding trends from a designer's POV — what's tired, what's rising, what looks expensive vs. generic right now. High comment/share driver.
- **Audience:** Both
- **Metrics goal:** Comments + Shares
- Source: thewed.com, candicecoppola.com

### 9. "Of Course I'm a Wedding Designer" Trend Format
Inserting specific design philosophy into the viral format: "Of course I obsess over the candle temperature before every event." Shows professional identity through specificity. High share rate — planners tag each other, couples tag their planners.
- **Audience:** Both (humor + desire)
- **Metrics goal:** Shares
- Source: TikTok trend discovery, multiple planner accounts

## Hook Patterns That Stop the Scroll

### Pattern A — The Design Ick (Fear hook)
*"These are the things that will make your wedding look like everyone else's."*
*"Stop doing this at your wedding if you want it to feel elevated."*
*"My wedding design icks — here's what I'd never let a couple do."*
- Engine: Fear of the generic; fear of bad taste. Strongest short-term scroll-stopper.

### Pattern B — The Curiosity Gap (Desire hook)
*"Watch what we turned this empty warehouse into..."* (visual: blank space)
*"Nobody talks about this, but this one detail is what separates a luxury wedding from an average one."*
*"The client said the budget was tight. Here's what we did anyway."*
- Engine: Withheld payoff. Drives completion rate above all other hook types.

### Pattern C — Bold Statement First Slide (for carousels)
*"Your wedding doesn't need more flowers. It needs a stronger concept."*
*"The most expensive-looking weddings aren't the ones with the biggest budget."*
*"Three things that will make your event look cheap — no matter how much you spent."*
- Engine: Polarizing/validating opinion. Drives tap-through to see the argument.

### Pattern D — Label/Callout (Audience qualifier hook)
*"If you're planning a wedding in 2026 and you don't want it to look like every other wedding at your venue —"*
*"For couples who want their guests to feel something, not just attend something."*
- Engine: Makes the right person feel instantly seen. Filters out the wrong audience automatically.

### Pattern E — The Real Story Opener
*"My first big event was in Oxford, England. I planned it from San Diego."*
*"$35k Hawaii wedding. Dry venue. We pulled it off."*
- Engine: Tension + specificity. Real stakes make people stay.

**Hard rule on hooks:** Lead with the VIEWER's dream or fear — not your credentials. Not "I'm a luxury wedding designer" but "You can have that wedding you keep saving on Pinterest. Here's what it actually takes."

## 2026 Aesthetic Trends (Aligned with This Brand)

### Macro trend: "Alt-Bride" / Anti-Traditional
Couples actively rejecting traditional wedding aesthetics in favor of expressive, personality-driven celebrations. Pinterest 2026 trend lead: *"Couples aren't just planning weddings, they're rewriting them."*
- Unifying thread: **aesthetic intention** — the wedding as identity expression, not template

### Specific trend data (Pinterest search growth, 2026):
- Draped backdrop: +1,510%
- Plum and olive wedding: +1,380%
- Celestial whimsigoth: +1,330%
- Jazz club wedding: +1,115%
- Opalite aesthetic (iridescent, ethereal): +2,710%
- Speakeasy lounge: +225%
- Opera aesthetic (dramatic decor, luxury drapery): strongly rising
- Dark romantic: rising

### Color direction 2026:
Warm earthy tones dominant — dusty mauve, warm cocoa, sage, antique white, deep berry. Green in 50%+ of modern weddings. Palette language: "a painting in a European gallery, not a Pinterest board."

### Tablescape trend:
"2026 dinner tables will be unapologetically maximalist" (The Wed). Layered, abundant, sensory — stacked vessels, unconventional materials, dramatic linens, antique mix-match. Directly counter to the 2021–2023 clean minimalism wave.

### Immersive / multi-sensory design:
Couples are no longer planning events that look beautiful — they are planning events that *feel* like something. Dramatic lighting, scent design, immersive soundscapes, theatrical entrances, venue transformations. This is the industry articulating "designed not decorated" in survey data.
- Source: The Wed 2026, Zola 2026, The Knot 2026, nFocus Magazine

## Voice of Customer — What Couples Are Actually Saying

**"I don't want my wedding to look like every other wedding at that venue."**
Most-cited fear among design-conscious couples across forums and industry research. Couples explicitly searching for alternatives to Pinterest-template weddings.
- Hook language: "What makes your wedding look like everyone else's," "how to make your space feel like yours and not a catalog photo"

**60% of couples feel pressure between online inspiration and real budget (up 12% YoY)**
#1 pain point in Zola 2026. Couples saving aspirational content that costs 3–5x what they can spend. This is the anxiety that a design-forward planner (who knows where to spend vs. save) is uniquely positioned to address.
- Hook language: "What actually makes an event feel expensive is almost never what you think it is"

**90% of couples want a "highly personalized" wedding**
The Knot 2026. "Traditional, cookie-cutter weddings are giving way to celebrations centered on authenticity, intention, and personal expression."
- Hook language: "Your wedding should feel like you, not like a wedding"

**Couples choose planners on personality (50%) and responsiveness (52%), before portfolio**
The Knot 2026. Social media content that shows personality, design philosophy, and point-of-view closes the portfolio gap for a planner building toward luxury.

## Strategic Synthesis

**The core opportunity:** The 2026 wedding market is moving toward exactly what this brand already stands for — immersive, designed, emotionally intentional events that feel like an experience. This is not positioning against the market; it is positioning WITH the macro trend. The planner's job in content is to name that trend in her voice, teach what it means in practice, and demonstrate that she is the one who can execute it.

**The portfolio gap solution:** Taste and POV signal luxury before the portfolio does. Content that reveals *how the planner thinks about design* (what she approves, what she rejects, and why) signals luxury expertise more effectively than portfolio images from mid-budget events. Melissa Andre proves this model — her content shows her thinking, not just her completed events.

**The two-platform split:**
- TikTok: Reach and discovery (transformations, ick callouts, day-in-the-life, trends). Shorter, punchier, personality-forward.
- Instagram: Authority and saves (carousels, color stories, mood board → reality). More curated, more reference-worthy.

**The content equation for this brand:**
Specific design opinion (the hook) + real event proof (the receipt) + "designed not decorated" philosophy (the bridge) + what this means for the viewer's wedding (the reward)
